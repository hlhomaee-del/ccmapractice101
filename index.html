Think like a medical assistant.

This platform turns CCMA-style questions into concept maps: records, law and ethics, communication, pharmacology, injections, ECG, respiratory care, infection control, nutrition, scheduling, medical history, coding, and emergency response.

Use the flashcards first, then practice one question at a time. This version contains 171 practice/challenge questions and 71 concept flashcards, including new developmental-psychology and scheduling memory labs. The blue map shows where you are, green shows correct, red shows missed, and the study box lets you collect weak topics for later review.

Instructional design by Hilary Homaii. This student-learning platform was developed for educational review using CMA/CCMA test-plan topics and publicly available study resources as general content guides. All teaching structure, explanations, activities, questions, rationales, and instructional sequencing were independently designed/adapted for classroom use.

Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs

This tab teaches priority levels. It is separate from grief, Erikson, milestones, and scheduling.

👆Click each pyramid level. It will flip and show how that level appears on a test. Remember: bottom needs come first.
☁️

Stages of Grief

This tab teaches grief-stage language and order. It is not Maslow and not Erikson.

👆Click each bright grief cloud. The front gives the stage; the back shows how a patient might express it on a test. Remember the order: denial → anger → bargaining → depression → acceptance.

Growth, Developmental Milestones & Puberty Order

This tab teaches developmental order. It is separate from psychological conflict stages.

👆Click each developmental card. The front gives the order; the back gives the testing trap so students remember what comes first.

Erik Erikson: Psychosocial Development

This tab teaches psychosocial conflicts by age. It is different from grief stages and Maslow priorities.

👆Hover over the chart rows and study the age clue, conflict, and test language. Each row uses one bright color family across the row.

Scheduling Systems

This tab teaches appointment patterns. It belongs to office workflow, not psychology.

👆Click each scheduling card. The front shows the pattern; the back shows the exam clue. Watch for same time, arrival order, catch-up time, and similar visit type.
